TROLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOLOL - war cry of the bog trolls (disputed)




[PART 1: INTRODUCTION]

Jokes aside, the bog trolls are, in fact, nothing like the moderately hilarious morons that lurk in the dark corners
of the internet.  They are mean, green killing machines.  Organized in a loose tribal society, the bog trolls are a serious
threat to other races in the world of LFR, even though much of the fighting they do is amongst themselves.

[PART 2: NATURE]

Bog trolls are violently aggressive.  Their attacks are not very organized, but are always savage and often unprovoked.  Scouts from
other races, humans in particular, are commonly ordered to kill bog trolls on sight, lest the troll return with a rag-tag warband
seeking blood and sport.  This is often easier said than done, as bog trolls have an innate resistance to pain and are fearless
even in the face of impossible odds.  Some have theorized that this fearlessness is a rudimentary form of religion which gives
the bog trolls their loose societal ties, but the more common claim is that the creatures are simply savage beasts who value the
spilling of blood over their own individual well being.  Regardless of their motives, if they can even be called motives, bog trolls will attack
any creature that encroaches on their native marshland territories, and will move on to neighboring civilizations when the supply
of passers by is exhausted.  Although they are certainly savage and by no means strategists, bog trolls are not entirely stupid.  They
are smart enough to open doors and dismantle structures, and seem to be, oddly enough, gifted farmers.  Though their fights are
frequent, bog trolls tend to avoid killing each other whenever possible.  Their natural lifespan is short: only about 60 years
on average.

[PART 3: SOCIETY]

What is known about bog troll society is that it is anything but complex.  Bog trolls can generally be found doing one of three things:
farming, fighting, or moving between farming and fighting.  Heirarchy amongst the trolls is generally established by size and strength,
though authority, in its limited role, is constantly being challenged.  The most successful bog troll leaders have succeeded in quelling
disputes over authority with force.  Bog troll language is simple an unintelligible to outsiders.

Bog trolls make no distinction between males and females.  The sexes are identical in size, and all are indiscriminate fighters.  It can
be difficult to tell from looking at them that there are two sexes at all.

Due to their ancestral habitat, bog trolls are naturally immune to most swamp-borne diseases.  Bog troll health care is virtually 
nonexistant, and trolls wounded in battle are either left to die or recover on their own.  Those that do recover are treated with an
unusual degree of respect by other individuals.  A bog troll in a fight with a battle-scarred rival will avoid striking at areas
where previous wounds were sustained.  Oddly enough, bog trolls are gifted farmers.

[PART 4: BATTLE TACTICS]

Physically, these creatures are roughly five times the size of a dwarf, and their skin is tougher than treated leather.  Like their punier cousins 
who live in a coexistance with the goblins, bog trolls have large, menacing tusks and are mountains of muscle.  They are naturally skilled in
unarmed combat, though this does not make them any less formiddable.  Bog trolls are not great craftsmen, but have been known to cobble
together makeshift weaponry and armor from items looted off the corpses of victims whose skulls had already been crushed.

Bog troll assaults are common, but rarely exceed small groups.  Full-scale wars and sieges are only fought in direct defense of their homelands
or in extremely rare circumstances, when a particularly strong and ruthless "boss" gathers a sufficiently large amalgamation of followers.  Because 
of their size and ferocity, most other races fear and attack them, but few are bold enough to attempt to exterminate them altogether.  An assault
on a bog troll homeland, even though such a place has no fortifications to speak of, would be a long and costly conflict that, thus far, no civiliation
has been willing to follow through with.  With no diplomatic or military efforts in the works, the bog trolls are just another danger of the untamed 
wilds to be dealt with cautiously.  Individually strong, their assaults on a fortress are disruptive and lethal to unprepared dwarves, but are usually 
outmatched by a moderately sized, geared, and trained military.

[PART 5: DIPLOMATIC RELATIONS]

Human and elven lords see little to gain from bog trolls' home territories, and dwarven settlements are not under enough of a threat to justify the loss of life 
that a campaign would all but guarantee.  The meowkin dislike the bog trolls, as they are ugly, large, and make poor slaves, and the voidwalkers seem to 
ignore them altogether.  The bog trolls harass the trade routes of any nearby civilizations, but give voidwalker territories a wide berth.  Whether this is
evidence of common sense among bog trolls or if the trolls who did not have this reservation were simply wiped out is a point of debate.
